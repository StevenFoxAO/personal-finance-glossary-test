name: Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)
slug: required-minimum-distribution
categories:

lastUpdated: 2026-04-22
plainEnglish: >-
  The minimum amount the IRS requires you to withdraw each year from certain
  retirement accounts once you reach a certain age, so it can finally collect
  income tax on that money.
detailedExplanation: >-
  RMDs apply to most tax-deferred retirement accounts — Traditional IRAs, 401(k)s,
  403(b)s, and similar — once the account owner reaches the RMD age. Under the
  SECURE 2.0 Act, RMD age is 73 [verify current age], scheduled to rise to 75 in
  2033. The required amount each year is calculated by dividing the account
  balance (as of December 31 of the prior year) by a life-expectancy factor from
  IRS tables. Missing an RMD historically triggered a 50% excise tax; SECURE 2.0
  reduced that to 25%, or 10% if corrected promptly [verify]. Roth IRAs owned by
  the original account holder are not subject to lifetime RMDs.
workedExample: >-
  An account balance of $500,000 with an IRS life-expectancy factor of 26.5 (a
  typical factor at age 73) produces an RMD of about $18,868 for that year — which
  you'd withdraw and include in taxable income.
usedInSentence: "\"I started taking my RMDs this year, so I adjusted my tax withholding to avoid an underpayment penalty.\""
whenItApplies: >-
  Holders of tax-deferred retirement accounts at or above RMD age, and
  beneficiaries who inherit retirement accounts (rules differ).
commonMistakes: >-
  Missing the deadline and owing the excise tax; forgetting that RMDs must be
  calculated per-IRA (though they can be aggregated for withdrawal); not planning
  for the tax impact of RMDs stacking on top of Social Security income.
howToRemember: >-
  "R-M-D: Really Must Distribute." The IRS stops letting you defer forever.
whenToConsult: >-
  A CPA or CFP® can model Roth conversions before RMDs start, calculate the RMD
  correctly for the first year (which has special deadline rules), and coordinate
  with charitable giving strategies like Qualified Charitable Distributions.
faqs:
  - question: At what age do RMDs start?
    answer: "Currently 73 [verify current age], rising to 75 in 2033 under SECURE 2.0."
  - question: Do RMDs apply to Roth IRAs?
    answer: Not during the original owner's lifetime. Inherited Roth IRAs may have distribution requirements.
  - question: What happens if I miss one?
    answer: The IRS charges an excise tax on the amount not taken. It can be reduced if corrected quickly and properly.
relatedTerms:
