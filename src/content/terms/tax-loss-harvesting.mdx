name: Tax-Loss Harvesting
slug: tax-loss-harvesting
categories:
  - taxes
  - investing-markets
lastUpdated: 2026-04-22
plainEnglish: >-
  Deliberately selling an investment at a loss so you can use that loss to offset
  taxable gains (or a small amount of ordinary income). You then typically
  reinvest the proceeds in something similar to stay in the market.
detailedExplanation: >-
  When investments drop below your cost basis in a taxable account, realizing
  those losses creates a tax benefit. The realized losses first offset any
  realized gains of the same type (long vs. short), then any gains of the other
  type, and up to $3,000 of remaining losses can offset ordinary income per year
  — with any excess carried forward indefinitely. The key rule: the wash-sale
  rule disallows the loss if you buy the "substantially identical" security
  within 30 days before or after the sale (in any account you or your spouse
  controls, including IRAs). Most harvesters swap into a similar but not
  identical fund to maintain exposure without triggering the rule.
workedExample: >-
  You sell an index fund at a $5,000 loss and buy a different (not substantially
  identical) index fund the same day. You've kept your stock exposure, and the
  $5,000 loss can offset a $5,000 gain elsewhere — saving up to $1,190 in tax at
  a 23.8% combined rate (20% long-term + 3.8% NIIT).
usedInSentence: "\"The market drop was brutal, but I harvested enough losses to wipe out my gains for the year.\""
whenItApplies: >-
  Only in taxable brokerage accounts. Losses inside IRAs and 401(k)s don't
  produce deductions.
commonMistakes: >-
  Triggering the wash-sale rule by buying the same fund in an IRA within 30
  days; harvesting losses in low-income years where you wouldn't have owed tax
  anyway; over-trading and racking up costs that exceed the tax benefit.
howToRemember: >-
  "Turn red ink into a receipt." The loss itself is real — harvesting just lets
  you get a tax benefit from it.
whenToConsult: >-
  A CPA or CFP® is worth consulting before a large harvest — especially if you
  hold multiple share lots, use automatic reinvestment, or have cross-account
  positions that could trigger a wash sale.
faqs:
  - question: What's the wash-sale rule?
    answer: You can't claim a loss if you (or your spouse) buy a substantially identical security within 30 days before or after the sale.
  - question: How much can losses reduce ordinary income?
    answer: Up to $3,000 per year, with the remainder carried forward to future years.
  - question: Does this work for crypto?
    answer: "The wash-sale rule currently doesn't apply to crypto [verify — Congress has considered changing this], though that can change."
