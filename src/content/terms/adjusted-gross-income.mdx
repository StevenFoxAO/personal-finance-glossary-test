---
name: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
slug: adjusted-gross-income
categories:
  - taxes
lastUpdated: 2026-04-22
plainEnglish: >-
  Your total taxable income for the year minus a specific list of "above-the-line"
  deductions the IRS allows. AGI is the jumping-off point for most of the rest of
  your tax return.
detailedExplanation: >-
  Gross income includes wages, interest, dividends, [capital gains](/term/capital-gains-tax), business
  income, and most other income. AGI equals gross income minus certain specific
  adjustments — things like [Traditional IRA](/term/traditional-ira) deductions, HSA contributions,
  student loan interest, and self-employment tax (half). AGI matters far beyond
  your tax bill: it's used to determine eligibility for [Roth IRA](/term/roth-ira) contributions,
  education tax credits, the premium tax credit for ACA health coverage, and
  many other thresholds. Modified AGI (MAGI) adds certain items back in and is
  the version used for some of those tests.
workedExample: >-
  You earned $120,000 in wages, had $2,000 of dividends, contributed $4,000 to an
  HSA through payroll, and paid $1,500 in student-loan interest. If the HSA
  contribution is already excluded from your W-2 wages, your gross income is
  $122,000, and your AGI is $122,000 − $1,500 = $120,500.
usedInSentence: "\"I need to check our AGI before we make a direct Roth contribution — we might be over the limit.\""
whenItApplies: >-
  Anyone filing a U.S. federal tax return. AGI appears on Form 1040 and drives
  dozens of downstream calculations.
commonMistakes: >-
  Confusing AGI with taxable income (taxable income is AGI minus the standard or
  itemized deduction and any QBI deduction); forgetting that MAGI can be higher
  than AGI for certain tests.
howToRemember: >-
  "Gross minus grown-up deductions." AGI is what the IRS starts with before
  applying most other rules.
whenToConsult: >-
  A CPA if any income-based credits, deductions, or premium subsidies are near
  their phase-out ranges — small moves in AGI can produce outsized swings in
  taxes owed.
faqs:
  - question: Where do I find my AGI?
    answer: On line 11 of Form 1040 [verify current line number — the IRS occasionally renumbers lines].
  - question: Is AGI the same as taxable income?
    answer: No. Taxable income is AGI minus your standard or itemized deduction (and any QBI deduction).
  - question: What is MAGI?
    answer: Modified AGI — your AGI with certain items added back, used for tests like Roth eligibility and IRMAA Medicare surcharges.
relatedTerms:
  - marginal-tax-rate
  - capital-gains-tax
  - roth-ira
