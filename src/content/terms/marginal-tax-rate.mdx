---
name: Marginal Tax Rate
slug: marginal-tax-rate
categories:
  - taxes
lastUpdated: 2026-04-22
plainEnglish: >-
  The tax rate you pay on your next dollar of income — not the average rate across
  all your income.
detailedExplanation: >-
  The U.S. federal income tax is progressive: different slices of your income are
  taxed at different rates, which step up as you earn more. Your marginal rate
  is the rate applied to the highest slice — the last dollar you earned. Your
  effective (average) rate is your total tax divided by your total income, and
  it's always lower than your marginal rate because the lower slices are taxed
  at lower rates. Marginal rates matter for decisions at the edge: whether a
  Roth conversion makes sense, whether an extra dollar of work is worth it,
  whether a Traditional vs. Roth contribution is better.
workedExample: >-
  Suppose the brackets are 10%, 12%, 22% and 24% [verify current brackets]. If
  your taxable income lands you in the 24% bracket, only the dollars above the
  24%-bracket threshold are taxed at 24%. A $10,000 bonus would be taxed at 24%
  federally = $2,400, not your entire income.
usedInSentence: "\"My marginal rate is 24%, but my effective rate is closer to 16%.\""
whenItApplies: >-
  Any decision involving "how much more tax will I owe on this extra dollar?" —
  earning more, doing a Roth conversion, realizing gains, etc.
commonMistakes: >-
  Thinking a raise can "push your whole income into a higher bracket" and
  actually leave you with less take-home (it almost never does for federal
  income tax — only the dollars above the threshold face the higher rate);
  confusing marginal and effective rates.
howToRemember: >-
  "Margin = the edge." Your marginal rate is the rate at the edge of your income
  pile.
whenToConsult: >-
  A CPA when income spikes (bonuses, equity vests, business sales) — the
  interaction between marginal rate, capital gains rate, NIIT, AMT, and state
  tax can get complicated fast.
faqs:
  - question: Does a higher bracket apply to all my income?
    answer: No. Only the income above that bracket's threshold is taxed at the higher rate.
  - question: Is my effective rate my marginal rate?
    answer: No. Effective rate is total tax / total income and is almost always lower than marginal.
  - question: Do state taxes have brackets too?
    answer: Most states do, though a handful use a flat rate or have no state income tax.
